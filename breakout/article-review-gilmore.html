THE REVIEW
HAPPY GILMORE






Happy Gilmore, Directed by Dennis Dugan, is a 90’s Romantic thriller about an old woman who couldn’t pay her taxes and was forced to work as a slave in an old people’s home where she falls into an abusive and violent relationship with the manager of the care home, and CEO of the quilt knitting sweat shop Hal, played by Ben Stiller. Frances Bay gives an incredibly weak performance as ‘feeble old tax dodger’ throughout the film. However, her character develops as she is abandoned by her grandson Happy [Played by Adam Sandler] who puts her in an old people’s home after she refused to pay government taxes. We see from the beginning of the film her distaste for her grandson as she terrifies him by wearing a clown mask and jumping out at Happy when he is forced to move into her home after his father was killed and mother estranged. We see Happy’s rage against his grandmother through his treatment of old people in the film. Upon arrival at the old peoples home, Happy who was overwhelmed with joy as to never deal with grandma again, decided to run over ‘crazy lady’, this was of course done by Dugan to depict Happy’s scarred past of long-term abuse endured at the wrinkly old hands of his Grandmother.

The film takes a dark turn when Care home warden Hal [Stiller] forces Grandma into slave labour at the care home, the director’s cut features long strenuous scenes where Stiller violently rapes grandma and physically abuses many other of the care home characters at the home; Phillis who was working in the sweatshop and complained that her ‘fingers hurt’ was heavily beaten by Stiller who continued to drag her out the room. Sounds of rape and torture of Phillis can be heard while grandma continues to knit harder as Phillis’s screams grow louder, with an incredible and apparently improvised scene by Stiller, we see the Meet the Fockers actor roam back into the sweatshop completely naked, dripping with sweat and blood, he then marches over to grandma, tweaks his handlebar moustache, which is dripping with frothed saliva, and calmly says “you want some of this? Do ya Grandma?”. The scene ends with applause from all crew and cast. The film then progresses into a love affair between Grandma and Stiller where Dugan questions the validity of a captive relationship, drawing inspiration from Nazi Concentration camps and the SS officers who had relationships with their Jewish prisoners. By the end of the film Happy [Sandler] is able to buy grandma’s house back after he signs a lucrative deal with Subway after winning a golf tournament. However, when it came to the editors table, producers wanted to see a more family friendly film, the rape scenes of Phillis and Grandma were cut [arguably Stiller’s career performance] and a more heavy focus was put on Sandler’s character Happy Gilmore and his golfing fast food franchise. However, many still prefer the original gritty version where we see, at the end of the film, grandma die of syphilis in her big useless house.

Movie Rating – 3.5/5
