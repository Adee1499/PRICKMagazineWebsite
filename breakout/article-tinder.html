Love at first swipe

L



It’s late at night, you’ve just had a dinner, you’re looking through Instagram and you see your mates all in relationships, you haven’t met anyone recently, who you like, and you wonder why? You need a bit of affirmation in your life, and you know where to find it. You go to the app store, and reluctantly punch into your 4-inch keypad, ‘T-I-N-D-E-R’. 170 megabytes and 5 minutes later of filling in your unimpressive personal information and filtered through your best holiday pics – you’re live. It’s been 1 hour now; you’ve flicked through more singles than you’ve met in the last 2 years, you’re thumb is aching with rejection until you’re finally all out of ‘swipes’. The ‘swipe’ is the notion that you could find your desirable other half through the flick of your index finger or the tap of your thumb, romantic I know. After filtering through their beach pics as well as the few ‘out out’ snaps with the ‘gals’ or ‘lads’, you think – fuck me – I’ve found the one. Two wanks later and a glass of water you’ve plucked up the courage to finally message them. Its awkward, you’ve never met them, but their pictures and patter aren’t bad. Maybe you’ll meet up? Actually, maybe not. Is this too weird? You don’t know but you haven’t had sex in months and haven’t been in a relationship even longer, you go for it. You arrange a date. They’re nothing like their pictures but their chat is above average for someone who’s a complete stranger. After a few more drinks you’re both pissed enough to courageously ask them to come back to yours, or vice versa. After some fairly average sex and an awkward goodbye, as well as a restless night sleep, you’re done. The ordeal is over, and you’ve been able to satisfy your natural instinctual desire for a quick shag. You don’t know if you’ll ever see them again, and you don’t really care.

This, ladies and gents, is the sad reality of dating today. It’s hardly Romeo and Juliette and its definitely nothing for Sting to scream about in his new album, but it’s what we’ve got. But hold on, isn’t that exactly the problem? Internet dating isn’t romantic or special, it’s not looking across the room of crowded people and seeing someone you feel completely blown back by, someone you feel compelled and excited at the possibility of speaking to. But no. Online dating has reduced these beautiful and special moments to a 2D image on your smart phone, encompassing no aspect of individuality, or even a slither of personality or charisma which one may attain through meeting someone in the flesh. Tinder is ruining and completely exploiting our desire of what it is to first meet someone you may really fancy. I mean, who can remember a time when you met a complete stranger who you immediately felt a connection with? Butterflies in your stomach just at the possibility of speaking to them, or the deep pain in your gut after being shot down? Ye, me neither. this vital part of social human interaction has now become a thing of the past. The organic, or ‘old fashioned’ way of meeting someone you really fancy is gone. Love at first sight, is dead.

Maybe this is why shows like First Dates and Married at First Site are becoming increasingly popular in the UK, as it shows a true depiction of what is [or isn’t] genuine excitement and spark at meeting a random person and having to socialise with them, playing with your compatibility in a way which is alien for most of the dating world today, hence making such good television. However, this might be a greater issue of vulnerability and insecurity of our generation, people are more afraid to put themselves out there without seeing and judging little photos and making small talk before ever thinking of physically introducing yourself. It also shows a deeper and ever-growing need of our generation, affirmation.

Today young people have become so obsessed with judgement and how we look, we are constantly barraged with updates on our phones of ‘beautiful’ skinny models on yachts, or rich muscular men in sports cars, or that person who is 2 years younger than you but is already a multi-millionaire with 4 cars and 12 girlfriends. Making you feel; ugly, sad and worthless. This then drums up huge demand for an app for you to find this affirmation that you so hungrily desire. You stay single because you don’t really know how to approach someone in an organic sense, you don’t even have much sex, but you do have people interested in you, and that’s all that matters? Right? Unfortunately. No. You’re still alone. When your phone runs out of charge and ‘Don’ or ‘Tarquin’ is unable to message you, you feel lonely and bored. But little did you know, you’ve been lonely and bored all along, the app is only taking your mind off it and filling this fickle hole with pixels of artificially created boyfriends that aren’t actually there, and most likely, never will be. Hopefully after we’re allowed out of our houses, we will re-discover joyful human interaction and delete these horrific and isolating apps which have turned us into anti-social hermits. So finally we might be able to seek REAL interactions with REAL people which will make us re-discover the sensation of REAL relationships. But until then. Fuck love, I’ve got tinder.
